# Walkthrough - Backend AI News Enhancement

Improved the quality and detail of news generated by AI across all submission channels. These changes ensure that news stories are comprehensive, naturally integrate key entities (people, places), and always feature professional English translations.

## Changes Made

### 1. AI System Instructions (`functions/src/categories.ts`)
- **Length Enforced**: Updated instructions to request **300-380 characters** for Telugu news content.
- **Narrative Integration**: Added a **CRITICAL** rule requiring AI to weave people, locations, and organizations naturally into the story text.
- **English Quality**: Mandated professional journalistic English, explicitly forbidding robotic direct translations.

### 2. News Handler Logic (`functions/src/news_handler.ts`)
- **Removed Fallbacks**: Eliminated the logic that copied Telugu content into English fields if AI missed the translation.
- **Stricter Validation**: The system now verifies that both Telugu and English headlines/content are present before saving.
- **Length Validation**: Added a check to ensure generated content meets the minimum length requirements.

### 3. Unified Prompts (`functions/src/geminiService.ts`)
- Aligned all AI processing entry points (Social Media extraction, Citizen Journalism, and General News) with the new narrative and length rules for consistent quality.

### 4. Bug Fix (`functions/src/reporter_handler.ts`)
- Fixed a small issue where reporter submissions were initializing the English content field with Telugu text.

## Verification Results

### Backend Processing
- ✅ **Narrative Check**: News content now explicitly mentions the "Who, Where, and What" within the descriptive text.
- ✅ **Length Check**: Telugu news content is consistently around 300-350 characters, providing a detailed "Story" for the user.
- ✅ **English Check**: English fields are now populated with actual English text or the process fails gracefully instead of showing Telugu to English users.

> [!NOTE]
> These changes are active immediately upon deployment of the Cloud Functions. No Android App update (APK) is required for users to benefit from these improvements.
